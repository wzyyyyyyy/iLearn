# Product

## Register

product

## Users

iLearn serves Jilin University students and learners who already have access to course material on the official platform. They open the app during study sessions, between classes, or when preparing offline review material. Their context is task-oriented: find a course, watch the right recording, download material reliably, and resume local learning without fighting the official web UI.

## Product Purpose

iLearn exists to make course video study, downloading, and offline review calmer and more dependable across Windows, macOS, and Linux. Success means the app feels trustworthy after login, keeps the user's place in the workflow, explains what is happening, and makes repeated actions such as searching, selecting, downloading, and opening files feel immediate.

## Brand Personality

Quiet, capable, and refined. The interface should feel like a mature study tool rather than a marketing page or a themed school portal. It should be calm enough for long sessions, direct enough for daily use, and polished enough that users trust it with course workflows.

## Anti-references

Avoid AI-template product UI: oversized dark sidebars, generic blue buttons, endless white cards, decorative gradients, nested cards, and fake dashboard furniture. Avoid official school website aesthetics: administrative, table-heavy, dated, and visually noisy. Avoid over-cool tool aesthetics: dark glass, glowing effects, heavy animation, or visual tricks that distract from learning.

## Design Principles

1. Keep the task in front: navigation and controls should make the next study action obvious.
2. Build quiet confidence: use restrained color, stable spacing, and clear states instead of decoration.
3. Respect long sessions: typography, contrast, and density must stay comfortable for sustained reading and scanning.
4. Make feedback immediate: loading, empty, error, and success states should be visible but not sticky or loud.
5. Reuse one vocabulary: the same button, list, card, panel, and notification language should appear across every screen.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. Use motion only for quick state feedback and keep it optional through platform reduced-motion behavior where possible. Do not rely on color alone for status. Controls should have predictable keyboard behavior, clear focus affordances, and comfortable hit targets for desktop use.
